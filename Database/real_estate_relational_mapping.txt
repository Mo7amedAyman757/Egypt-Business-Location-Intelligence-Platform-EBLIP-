SECTION 3 — MAPPING DECISIONS & NOTES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A. 1:1 RELATIONSHIP (Location ↔ Demographics)
   - Location_ID acts as BOTH Primary Key and Foreign Key in DEMOGRAPHICS.
   - No separate surrogate key needed.
   - This ensures exactly one demographics record per location.

B. 1:N RELATIONSHIPS
   - All 1:N relationships are resolved by placing the FK on the "many" side.
   - Example: One Location → Many University_Student records.
     → Location_ID is added as FK to UNIVERSITY_STUDENTS.

C. ENUMERATED ATTRIBUTES (stored as VARCHAR with CHECK constraints)
   - Property_Type: Apartment | Shop | Clinic | Villa | Land | Office
   - Listing_Type:  Rental | Sale | Commercial_Rent
   - Price_Period:  monthly | total

D. DUAL COORDINATES
   - LOCATION has Latitude/Longitude representing the general area.
   - PROPERTY also has Latitude/Longitude for the specific property location.
   - Both use DECIMAL(10,6) for high precision (as noted in original ERD).

E. DUAL FK IN COMPETITOR
   - COMPETITOR references both LOCATION (where?) and BUSINESS_TYPE (what?).
   - This avoids redundancy — business types are normalized into their own table.

F. NO WEAK ENTITIES
   - No weak entities were identified in this ERD.
   - All entities have their own primary keys.

G. NO MULTI-VALUED ATTRIBUTES
   - No attribute requires a separate decomposition table.
   - Property_Type and Listing_Type are single-valued (one per record).

H. "REAL ESTATE MARKET" ANNOTATION
   - The dashed-border "Real Estate Market" box in the original ERD is a
     design annotation / story note — NOT an entity.
   - It was NOT mapped to a table.

I. ALL PRICES IN EGP
   - As noted in the original ERD, all numeric prices are stored in EGP.
